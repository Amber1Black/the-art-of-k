Why cake is called "cake"?

The term "cake" has a very long history, it traces back through several languages and centuries. The word "cake" comes from the Old Norse word "kaka." The Old Norse "kaka" referred to a small, flat bread or loaf. This word was then translated into Old English as "cāc," which eventually evolved into the modern English word "cake."

Originally, cakes were more like bread and were often sweetened with honey. Over time, as baking techniques and ingredients improved, cakes became lighter and sweeter, resembling the desserts we know today.
